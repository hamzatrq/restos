| DEC-PERF-001 | Ordering authority: cloud-assigned `global_seq` vs stable append-time order | proposed | 25 (full analysis) | Adopting a cloud-assigned sequence retroactively reorders events, which forces a full re-fold — **measured O(N²)** on the live sync path (~6.6 ms/event at N=1600 on a laptop; extrapolates to minutes of CPU on a 2 GB tablet at a realistic rolling window). External research verdict: general IVM engines (DBSP/Materialize) do **not** solve this — they are order-agnostic, and DBSP's own degenerate case is exactly ours. Options in `25 §7`: cheap wins (skip no-op adoptions, entity-scoped recompute, batch per page) are decision-free. **Structural recommendation now settled in `25 §11–§16`: a clock-free causal order** — key `(causal_seq, device_id)` where `causal_seq` is a true Lamport clock bumped on append AND receive, assigned once and never revised; `global_seq` demoted to a delivery/catch-up cursor. Chosen over HLC because the founder's clock threat model allows EVERY device to be arbitrarily wrong in either direction, which removes HLC's premise (it needs some roughly-right clock) while keeping its hazard (a fast clock poisons the org's monotone max irrecoverably).
